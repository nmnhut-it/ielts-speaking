The Exam

What is IELTS Speaking?

The IELTS Speaking test is a face-to-face interview with an examiner lasting 11 to 14 minutes. It has three parts, each testing different skills.

  • Part 1: Interview (4-5 min) General questions about yourself, your home, work, studies, and familiar topics.
  • Part 2: Long Turn (3-4 min) You receive a cue card with a topic. One minute to prepare, then speak for up to two minutes.
  • Part 3: Discussion (4-5 min) Abstract questions related to the Part 2 topic. Requires deeper analysis and opinion.
Total duration: 11-14 minutes
Format: One-on-one with an examiner
Scored on: Fluency & Coherence, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range & Accuracy, Pronunciation
Band scale: 0 to 9 (half-band increments)

Scoring

How You'll Be Scored

Fluency & Coherence Speak smoothly without long pauses. Connect your ideas logically using linking words.
Lexical Resource Use varied vocabulary. Avoid repeating the same words. Use idiomatic expressions naturally.
Grammatical Range & Accuracy Use a mix of simple and complex sentence structures. Minimize errors.
Pronunciation Speak clearly. Use natural stress and intonation. Be understood without effort.
